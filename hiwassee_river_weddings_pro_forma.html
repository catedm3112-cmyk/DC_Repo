Buyer-side interpretation: The operating cash flow appears strong on paper if 75 weddings at approximately $7,500 average revenue are sustainable. However, the seller proposal creates significant refinance risk because the buyer must solve a roughly $1.56M payoff within only 24 months.

Key Buyer-Side Concerns

  • Two-year balloon is the largest economic risk. It may not allow enough time to build transferable operating history for permanent commercial financing.
  • 20-year amortization increases monthly debt service compared with a 30-year amortization and reduces transition-period cash-flow flexibility.
  • Seller retaining title until payoff is materially less favorable to the buyer than receiving deed/title at closing with the seller secured by a deed of trust or mortgage.
  • Cash-flow assumptions depend on verification — verified bookings, actual historical P&L, event expense load, insurance, flood policy cost, taxes, and maintenance reserves.

Preliminary Conclusion

The proposed $250,000 down payment is attractive, but the 2-year balloon and seller-retained title structure are major buyer risks. From a buyer perspective, it would be reasonable to sacrifice some interest rate or even some down payment if needed to secure a longer balloon, deed/title at closing, 30-year amortization, clear transfer of bookings/deposits, and strong transition support from the seller.
